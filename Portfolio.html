01 — Featured Project
AI or Animal?
From Museum to Zoo: Adapting a Generative AI Literacy Framework for Informal Learning in a Novel Context
?

The Problem

People see Generative AI as a bane rather than a boon, and learning from a classroom setting is not accessible to everyone. Parents often misinform children about both animals and AI technologies. While museum-based AI exhibits exist, zoological parks remain an untapped space for informal AI literacy.

!

The Solution

A gamified, web-based interactive kiosk called "AI or Animal?" that challenges visitors to distinguish between real and AI-generated animal content, teaching Generative AI literacy and animal knowledge simultaneously through play, surprise, and immediate feedback.

Research Question 1

How can design elements of Generative AI exhibits affect visitors' understanding of AI concepts?

Research Question 2

What mental models do visitors construct when interacting with Generative AI exhibits in zoo settings?

02 — Design Process
End-to-End HCI Methodology
A rigorous, user-centered process from needfinding through evaluation, grounded in participatory design and iterative testing.
Phase 1 · Discovery

Needfinding & Literature Review

Identified the gap in informal AI learning at zoological parks through literature review of Long et al. (2021), Clay et al. (2011), and Webber (2015). Conducted preliminary needfinding to understand zoo visitor behaviors and information needs, revealing that parents often fabricate answers to children's questions about animals and technology.

Literature Review Needfinding Gap Analysis
Phase 2 · Define

Scenario-Based System Design

Developed detailed use cases and interaction scenarios: identifying AI-generated content (primary task) and learning quizzes (secondary task). Mapped actors, system requirements, preconditions, and postconditions for the kiosk experience — from a family browsing animal trivia to zoo staff performing maintenance.

Use Case Modeling Scenario Design User Journey Mapping
Phase 3 · Build

Rapid Prototyping

Built a functional web-based prototype using Claude AI as a development assistant. The game presents animal images and challenges users to identify whether they are real or AI-generated, with immediate feedback including fun facts, detection clues, and a progress bar tracking completion.

Web Prototyping HTML/CSS/JS Gamification Design
Phase 4 · Evaluate

Think-Aloud Usability Testing

Conducted think-aloud sessions with 9 participants (ages 6‚Äì35) across diverse technical backgrounds: children, artists, professors, data scientists, and general users. Used both concurrent and retrospective think-aloud protocols via in-person, WhatsApp, and Discord sessions.

Think-Aloud Protocol Pilot Testing Qualitative Analysis
Phase 5 · Analyze

Thematic Analysis

Analyzed think-aloud transcripts using thematic analysis (Creswell & Creswell, 2017). Identified 3 major themes and 13 sub-themes. Top sub-themes by participant count: Learning Value (8/9), Feedback Appropriateness (6/9), and Engagement Features (5/9).

Thematic Analysis Transcript Coding Data Saturation
Phase 6 · Iterate

Heuristic Evaluation & Redesign

Applied Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics with a co-evaluator. Identified the most critical violation: missing Help & Documentation (severity 4). Iterated on the design: removed broken audio, added gray-area images, prompt transparency, detection clues, and fit-to-screen layout.

Nielsen's Heuristics Severity Rating Design Iteration

Heuristic Evaluation Highlights

Nielsen's 10 Heuristics applied across both tasks. Severity scale: 0 (no issue) to 4 (catastrophe).

Help & Documentation
Severity: 4 ‚Äî Catastrophe

No help or support link exists. Users encountering terms like "prompt" or "artifact" have no way to access explanations.

User Control & Freedom
Severity: 2 ‚Äî Minor

Users must intentionally fail to see wrong-answer educational content; no way to explore all feedback paths voluntarily.

Recognition > Recall
Severity: 2 ‚Äî Minor

AI recognition strategies are static rather than tied to specific images. Dynamic, contextual clues would improve learning.

Visibility of Status
Severity: 1 ‚Äî Cosmetic

Progress bar glitches and image loading delays can inadvertently reveal whether content is AI-generated.
